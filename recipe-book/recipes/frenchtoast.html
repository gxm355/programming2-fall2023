French Toast

French toast, also known as "eggy bread," is a classic breakfast dish made by dipping slices of bread into a mixture of beaten eggs, milk, and often flavored with vanilla extract, cinnamon, or nutmeg. The coated bread slices are then cooked on a griddle or skillet until golden brown and slightly crispy on the outside, while remaining soft and fluffy on the inside.
French toast can be enjoyed in various ways. It can be served as is, with a dusting of powdered sugar or a drizzle of maple syrup. It pairs well with a variety of toppings such as fresh berries, sliced bananas, whipped cream, or even a dollop of yogurt. For a savory twist, some people prefer to top it with bacon, cheese, or herbs.
This delightful dish is not only delicious but also a fantastic way to use up stale bread as it helps revitalize its texture and flavors. French toast is a versatile option that can be enjoyed for breakfast, brunch, or as a sweet treat any time of the day.
With its crispy exterior, soft interior, and endless topping possibilities, French toast is a beloved comfort food that adds a touch of warmth and sweetness to any meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon Vanilla Extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon Ground Cinnamon
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 4 slices hearty sandwich bread

Instructions:

  1. Whisk egg, vanilla and cinnamon in shallow dish. Stir in milk.
  2. Dip bread slices in egg mixture, turning to coat evenly on both sides.
  3. Cook bread slices on lightly greased nonstick griddle or skillet on medium heat until cooked through and browned on both sides. Voila, easy French toast.
