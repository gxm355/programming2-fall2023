Tomato Soup

Tomato soup is a classic and comforting soup made primarily from ripe tomatoes. It is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed in various ways, whether as a light meal on its own or paired with a sandwich or salad.
To make tomato soup, ripe tomatoes are often blended or pureed to create a smooth and velvety texture. Other common ingredients include onions, garlic, vegetable or chicken broth, and seasonings such as salt, pepper, and herbs like basil or oregano. The soup is typically cooked over low heat to allow the flavors to meld together.
Tomato soup is known for its vibrant red color and its rich, slightly tangy taste. It is a favorite among many for its comforting and nostalgic appeal. The soup can be enjoyed hot or even chilled, depending on personal preference.
Tomato soup can be customized to suit individual tastes by adding various ingredients such as cream, butter, or cheese to enhance its richness and creaminess. It can also be garnished with fresh herbs, croutons, or a drizzle of olive oil for added flavor and texture.
Overall, tomato soup is a classic, versatile, and flavorful dish that brings warmth and comfort, perfect for a cozy meal, particularly during colder months.

Ingredients

  • Tomatoes (Ripe tomatoes are always best but canned whole tomatoes will also be delicious)
  • Balsamic vinegar
  • Olive oil
  • Salt, pepper, and sugar
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Basil leaves (Other herbs like thyme and oregano are also delicious)
  • Chicken Stock
  • Tomato paste
  • Cream

How to make homemade tomato soup

Roast the tomatoes:
  1. Roasting the tomatoes with aromatics before blending into the soup builds serious flavor. It’s a quick and easy step that means your soup goes from good to great. If you don’t have access to ripe, in-season tomatoes, using whole canned tomatoes are perfectly fine.
  2. Flavor the soup with ingredients that compliment tomatoes. Balsamic vinegar, basil and garlic are always a good idea where tomatoes are concerned. A pinch of chilli flakes/red pepper flakes will add a hum of heat if you like spicier foods.A little sugar tempers the acidity of the tomatoes but is completely optional.

    3. Make the soup:

    1. In a large pot or Dutch oven set over medium-high heat, cook the onions, garlic and basil in a little olive oil or butter until soft and translucent. Add the roasted tomatoes and tomato paste to this base along with stock/broth.
    2. Bring to a boil then cook until all the ingredients are soft then transfer to a blender and blend until smooth (alternatively use an immersion blender). Season to taste with salt and black pepper and add cream. Bring back up to a simmer then serve hot with bread of your choice or grilled cheese sandwiches.
